Why go to this hackathon?

Is the hackathon topic something for me?

Everyone is affected by violence.

Next to killing thousands of innocent people, acts of violence have given rise to even more unrest and exclusion across societies. So it’s a great time to ask questions: How can I help to prevent violence and contribute to a more inclusive society? By diving in to build something, many blocks and questions are encountered which wouldn’t otherwise be found.

You’ll also be surrounded by by other people with a similar drive to make a difference. And everyone has different pieces to the same puzzle – just by talking and working together, you will learn about other resources and initiatives out there.

The Challenge

What is the challenge about?

In this hackathon, we challenge participants to come up with ideas to
  1. Prevent violence
  2. Promote social inclusion
  3. Give a voice to people in areas struck by conflict or censorship
This broad formulation aims to keep the door open for innovative ideas beyond what we could think of.

To give you some guidance however, we include some examples of projects that we consider to be very interesting:
  • Twitter Speak-To-Tweet
    In 2011, Google and Twitter partnered up to create Speak-To-Tweet, in an effort to overcome internet blackouts in Egypt during anti-government protests there. The service allowed people without access to the Web share messages on Twitter via voice mail. For each call, the service instantly posted a message to Twitter with a link to listen to the message.
  • Amazon Alexa - implicit bias listener
    Most of us have implicit biases towards various social groups. One can picture an Amazon Alexa skill that listens select conversations and makes you aware of any implicit biases that come out of the transcript.

What are the judgement criteria?

Projects will be evaluated on the idea itself as much as on the technical implementation.

Key criteria are:
  1. Capability to prevent violence
  2. Capability to promote social inclusion
  3. Technical implementation

Do I need to know how to code?

Short answer: No! We are looking for practical ideas that enable the diffusion of violence or promotion of social inclusion.

On Sunday's jury, you can present a keynote or use prototyping tools like prototype.io or Balsamiq Mockups to convey your message.

Rules & Resources

Can I participate with a pre-formed team?

Yes you can participate in a pre-formed team. We will be looking for teams of 5-6 people, so you might need to accomodate other people in your group too.

How can I prepare for this hackathon?

A good preparation gets you a long way.

What you can do:
  • Brainstorm on an idea that you'd like to work on
  • Wireframe
  • Learn technologies
  • Think about your pitch and storyline of your demo
What you can not do:
  • Write code upfront

Does my team keep the intellectual property rights on the code?

We highly encourage a culture of sharing within the tech community. You are allowed however to restrict intellectual property on your code.
